#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

The dashboard layout and component structure are fundamentally solid, but the visual execution leans heavily into saturated AI-generated SaaS tropes. The welcome banner's combination of glowing blobs, dot grids, and gradient text conflicts with the "Minimalist, focused, structured" brand personality defined in the product strategy. The deterministic scan confirmed two issues: Space Grotesk is flagged as an overused AI display font, and there is a contrast warning for gray text on a colored background in the Admin panel.

#### Overall Impression
A functional and well-structured dashboard that undermines its own academic authority with unnecessary visual flair. The layout and IA are great, but the styling needs to be pulled back to match the serious tone of the SAT.

#### What's Working
- **Information Architecture**: The grouping of modules by folders and the clear metrics row makes the dashboard highly scannable.
- **Clear Hierarchy**: The separation of primary test content on the left and widgets on the right works perfectly for this use case.

#### Priority Issues

- **[P1] Overused AI SaaS Aesthetics**: The welcome banner's glowing blobs, dot grids, and gradient text feel like a generic SaaS template rather than a focused testing tool.
  - *Why it matters*: It breaks the "academic but encouraging" and "distraction-free" brand principles.
  - *Fix*: Strip the decorative blurs and dots. Rely on a clean, solid background with subtle depth or a restrained brand pattern.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable quieter`

- **[P2] Overused Typography**: The system pairs Space Grotesk (display) with Inter (body), which is a very common AI tell and unnecessary for a product UI.
  - *Why it matters*: Product UIs benefit from a single, highly legible family. Space Grotesk feels too stylized for a strict testing environment.
  - *Fix*: Standardize on Inter across the board for a tighter, more utilitarian aesthetic.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable typeset`

- **[P2] Missing Keyboard Accelerators**: The dashboard relies entirely on pointer interactions to navigate between sections and start tests.
  - *Why it matters*: Power users returning daily to practice will find the pointer-only navigation slow and repetitive.
  - *Fix*: Implement basic keyboard navigation (e.g., numbers to jump to sections, Enter to resume the most recent module).
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable harden`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**:
- No keyboard shortcuts to jump into the next recommended module.
- Must manually expand folders every time if they want to access older tests.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**:
- The gray text on colored backgrounds (flagged in the admin panel) and some lower-contrast secondary text (`text-slate-500` on white) may fail WCAG AA contrast requirements.

#### Minor Observations
- The "Làm lại" (Retry) button on attempted modules could be de-emphasized slightly to encourage users to focus on new modules first.
- The leaderboard ranking displays as "—" when null, which is fine, but could be replaced with a more encouraging empty state like "Chưa xếp hạng".

#### Questions to Consider
- Does the dashboard need a "Hero" section at all, or would users prefer jumping straight into their next module?
- How might the design celebrate progress without relying on glowing SaaS UI patterns?
