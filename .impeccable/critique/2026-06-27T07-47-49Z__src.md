#### Overall Impression
The functionality is solid (keyboard shortcuts, resizable panes, highlight tools), but the aesthetic is fundamentally mismatched. It defaults to an overly decorative, glassmorphic SaaS template instead of the strict, academic focus required by the Digital SAT context.

#### What's Working
- **Keyboard Navigation**: The inclusion of A/B/C/D and arrow key shortcuts in the testing interface is excellent for power users.
- **Split-Screen Paradigm**: The resizable left/right pane in the test screen successfully mimics the real Bluebook app's structural layout.

#### Priority Issues
- **[P1] Inconsistent Tone & Glassmorphism**: The UI uses translucent backgrounds, blurs (`backdrop-blur-md`), and gradient text, contradicting the "distraction-free" and "academic" brand principles.
  - *Why it matters*: It makes the app feel like a generic SaaS tool rather than a serious standardized test simulator.
  - *Fix*: Remove all `backdrop-blur` utilities, gradient text, and heavy use of `bg-color/10`. Use solid, neutral backgrounds for headers and toolbars.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable quieter`

- **[P1] Overused Typography**: Relying completely on Inter.
  - *Why it matters*: While legible, it contributes heavily to the "AI-generated" feel and lacks the specific academic character of a testing platform.
  - *Fix*: Switch to a more deliberate font pairing (e.g., a geometric sans for UI elements and a highly legible serif or robust sans for reading passages).
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable typeset`

- **[P2] Undocumented Colors**: 10 instances of arbitrary hex/rgba colors in `index.css` not defined in `DESIGN.md`.
  - *Why it matters*: Leads to visual inconsistency and makes theming (like Dark/Light mode) brittle.
  - *Fix*: Extract these into the design system variables or replace them with existing tokens.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable extract`
